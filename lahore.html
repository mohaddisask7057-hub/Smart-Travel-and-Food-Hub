History & Culture

Lahore is one of South Asia’s oldest cities, renowned for its Mughal-era architecture, rich traditions, poetry, music, and vibrant food culture. It served as a major capital during the Mughal Empire and remains Pakistan’s cultural and artistic hub.

Top Tourist Places

Badshahi Mosque

The Badshahi Mosque is a Mughal-era imperial mosque located in Lahore, Punjab, Pakistan. It was constructed between 1671 and 1673 during the rule of the Mughal emperor Aurangzeb, opposite to the Lahore Fort in the northern outskirts of the historic Walled CityOne of the largest mosques in the world, an architectural masterpiece from the Mughal era.

Lahore Fort

The Lahore Fort is a citadel in the walled interior of Lahore in Punjab, Pakistan. The fortress is located at the northern end of the Walled City and is spread over an area greater than 20 hectares. It contains 21 notable monuments, some of which date to the era of Emperor AkbarA UNESCO World Heritage Site showcasing royal palaces, halls, and Mughal artistry.

Shalimar Gardens

Beautiful terraced gardens built by Emperor Shah Jahan, perfect for evening walks.

Minar-e-Pakistan

Minar-e-Pakistan is a national monument located in Lahore, Punjab, Pakistan. The tower stands in the Greater Iqbal Park, an urban park in Lahore.A national monument symbolizing the birth of Pakistan, surrounded by lush parks.

Famous Foods of Lahore

Payee

"Payee" (or Paya) refers to a rich, slow-cooked South Asian stew or soup made from animal trotters (feet) of goat, lamb, cow, or buffalo, simmered for hours with aromatic spices to create a gelatinous, flavorful broth, often enjoyed as a hearty breakfast or special meal with naan or roti. A rich traditional breakfast dish slow-cooked overnight, loved across Lahore.

Halwa Puri

Halwa puri is a popular, hearty, vegetarian breakfast or brunch dish from the Northern Indian subcontinent, widely enjoyed in Pakistan, Bangladesh, and India. It is a complete meal that provides a balance of sweet, savory, and spicy flavors. A classic Lahori breakfast served with chana and sweet halwa.

Falooda

Falooda is a popular Mughlai cold dessert that is a cross between a sundae and a milkshake, widely enjoyed as a street food and summer treat across South Asia. It originated from the Persian dessert faloodeh and is typically served in a tall glass with various layered ingredients. A refreshing dessert made with milk, vermicelli, ice cream, and rose syrup.

Visitor Precautions

  • Summers are hot — wear light clothing and stay hydrated.
  • Air pollution can be high — carry a mask if needed.
  • Wear comfortable shoes for historical site visits.
  • Traffic can be heavy — plan travel times wisely.
  • Drink bottled water only.
