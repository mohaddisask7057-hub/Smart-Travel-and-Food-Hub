History & Culture

Karachi, Pakistan’s largest city and economic heart, has a rich history stretching back to ancient times. It has been ruled by many civilizations, including the Mauryans, Arabs, and British, forming a unique cultural blend of traditions, art, and modern urban energy.

Top Tourist Places

Clifton Beach

Karachi's Clifton Beach, also known as Sea View, is Pakistan's most famous and lively seaside spot, stretching along the Arabian Sea and bustling with locals enjoying camel/horse rides, buggy adventures, street food, and sunsets, though it's busy and generally not for swimming due to its strong currents, offering vibrant street life and picturesque views. Famous for its sunsets, camel rides, and seaside food stalls — perfect for families and couples.

Quaid-e-Azam Mausoleum

Mazar-e-Quaid, also known as Jinnah Mausoleum or the National Mausoleum, is the final resting place of Muhammad Ali Jinnah, the founder of Pakistan. Designed in a 1960s modernist style, it was completed in 1971, and is an iconic symbol of Karachi as well as one of the most popular tourist sites in the city.A peaceful white marble tomb of Pakistan’s founder, Muhammad Ali Jinnah, surrounded by gardens.

Mohatta Palace

Mohatta Palace is a museum of the fine and decorative arts of Pakistan and its collections permanent or temporary, reflect Pakistan's artistic traditions in stone, metal, clay, wood, cloth and paper. Set on a large estate, the Mohatta Palace was built in 1927 as the seaside home of Rai Bahadur Seth Shivratan Mohatta.An iconic museum showcasing art, culture, and history in a beautifully restored palace.

Famous Foods of Karachi

Karachi Biryani

Karachi Biryani is a popular, spicy, and masala-rich Pakistani rice dish known for its flavorful layering of tender meat (chicken, beef, or mutton), long-grain basmati rice, potatoes, fried onions, and a blend of aromatic whole and ground spices, often cooked in the dum style (slow-steamed) for deep, integrated flavors, creating a hearty, iconic meal. Aromatic spicy rice dish with tender meat — Karachi’s pride and comfort food staple.

Nihari

Karachi Nihari is a rich, spicy, slow-cooked meat stew (beef, mutton, or chicken) featuring bone marrow, thickened with flour, and flavored with aromatic spices like long pepper, traditionally served with naan for breakfast or as a hearty meal, known for its deep, tender flavors achieved by hours of simmering.Slow-cooked rich stew eaten with naan — perfect for mornings and hearty appetites.

Karachi BBQ

Karachi BBQ is known for its vibrant, spicy, and smoky Pakistani grilled meats, featuring tender marinated chicken (tikka, malai boti, bihari) and beef, often served with fresh naan, tangy chutneys (mint, tamarind), onions, and salad, with popular items like Seekh Kebabs, Karahi, and Paratha Rolls offering rich, flavorful, melt-in-your-mouth textures from charcoal grilling. Succulent grilled meats infused with spices — a must-try at street stalls and restaurants.

Visitor Precautions

  • Always carry a city map or offline guide.
  • Karachi gets hot — wear sunscreen and keep hydrated.
  • Use registered transport and follow traffic signals.
  • Drink bottled water only.
  • Stay in well-lit busy areas after dark.
