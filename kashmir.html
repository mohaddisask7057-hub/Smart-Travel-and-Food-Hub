History & Culture

Kashmir, often described as *“Heaven on Earth,”* is famous for its dramatic landscape of snow-capped mountains, serene lakes, and lush green valleys. It holds cultural significance with its cuisine, crafts, and traditions that reflect centuries-old heritage.Until the mid-19th century, the term Kashmir denoted only the Kashmir Valley between the Great Himalayas and the Pir Panjal Range. The term has since also come to encompass a larger area that formerly comprised the princely state of Jammu and Kashmir, and includes the Indian-administered territories of Jammu and Kashmir and Ladakh, the Pakistani-administered territories of Azad Kashmir and Gilgit-Baltistan.

Top Tourist Places

Neelum Valley

Neelum Valley is a stunning, lush valley in Pakistan-administered Kashmir, known for its pristine landscapes, the winding Neelum River, snow-capped peaks, and vibrant flora, making it a top tourist spot for nature lovers seeking adventure or tranquility, especially between April and October. A crown jewel valley known for riverside cottages, emerald waters and peaceful mountain views — perfect for nature lovers.

Ratti Gali Lake

Ratti Gali Lake is an alpine glacial lake which is located in Neelum Valley, Azad Kashmir, Pakistan. The lake is located at an altitude of 3,683 metres. The lake is fed by the surrounding glacier waters of the mountains.Alpine glacial lake at 12,130 ft, surrounded by snow peaks and bright flower meadows — a must-see adventure destination.

Dal Lake

Dal Lake, Srinagar's iconic urban lake known as the "Jewel of Kashmir," is famous for its scenic beauty, houseboats, and floating markets, offering shikara rides amidst Mughal gardens like Shalimar Bagh and Nishat Bagh, and is a central hub for tourism and local life in Jammu & Kashmir, India. High altitude glacial lake with snow-clad backdrop — breathtaking for campers and hikers alike.

Famous Foods in Kashmir

Rogan Josh

Aromatic slow-cooked lamb curry with rich Kashmiri spices — a signature dish.

Gushtaba

Meatballs simmered in creamy, spiced sauce — a highlight of local cuisine.

Kashmiri Pulao/Modur Pulav

Modur Polao ( Kashmiri sweet pulao) is a fragrant, flavorful, and mildly sweet rice dish originating from Kashmir.

Visitor Precautionary measures

  • Pack warm layers, especially in winter.
  • Carry sturdy boots for hikes around lakes and valleys.
  • Sunscreen & hats help at high altitudes.
  • Keep bottled water and snacks on longer treks.
  • Roads can be narrow — rent a local guide/driver.
